Degrees & Programs


FJU offers a number of educational experiences designed to address learner’s differing needs. These offerings are designed to provide dedicated learners a path to the knowledge, skills, professional behaviors, and credentials required by an ever-growing number of employers.


Courses: At the heart of these experiences are courses, the most common being the four-unit course. A four-unit course requires, on average, 160 hours of serious work by a dedicated learner. Unlike traditional schools where students take a number of classes at the same time, at FJU, students focus on one course at a time, full-time (averaging 40 hours per week) or part-time (averaging 20 or 10 hours a week). Depending on the time learners have agree to dedicate to their studies, full-time students will finish a four-unit course in four weeks and part-time students will spend eight or sixteen weeks to complete the same course.


Co-op: All programs at FJU require learners to successfully experience practical work either by means of group projects and/or apprenticeship at a participating corporation. Undergraduates in an approved co-op program will be expected to spend at least four months every year in a work place environment (possibly 3 to 4 hours every day). Learners with well-documented (e.g. a compelling portfolio) and approved work experiences (addressing the required skills) may petition to waive this co-op requirement.


Practicum: FJU requires learners to demonstrate what they have learned and are able to do by means of a practicum. There are 8-unit small practicums, requiring 320 hours of effort (roughly two months full-time), and 16-unit large practicums requiring 640 hours of effort (a semester of full-time effort). Unlike courses that require learners to acquire and practice new knowledge, practicums require the learner to apply what has been learned to real-world projects. A crucial aspect of a practicum is the learner’s ability to demonstrate that they are able to produce quality work products using appropriate processes, methods, and tools consistent with current professional best practices.


Badge: The smallest credential a learner may earn is a badge. Badges range from a single course up to an approved set of three, four-unit courses and can be earned with as little as one month of full-time effort. These are typically used as part of an employer’s or a professional’s continuing education and currency program.


Certificate: Certificates typically require an approved set of four, four-unit courses (a total of 640 hours) plus the successful completion of a small practicum project (an additional 320 hours). Certificates typically require a full-time learner to dedicate four months of effort (a semester) to the courses in the certificate and an additional two months on a small practicum project.


Associate’s Degree: A learner with a high school diploma, GED, or equivalent may earn this credential after four full-time semesters of approved study plus an approved co-op. This is typically accomplished by taking the equivalent of 16 four-unit courses plus the co-op. Half of the coursework covers potential job-related technical content while the remainder is dedicated to liberal arts courses aimed at helping the learner become an effective, successful, and satisfied member of society.


Bachelor’s Degree: A learner with a high school diploma, GED, or equivalent may earn this credential after eight full-time semesters of approved study plus an approved co-op. This is typically accomplished by taking the equivalent of 32 four-unit courses (including approved Project courses) and/or the co-op. Half of the coursework covers potential job-related technical content while the remainder is dedicated to liberal arts courses aimed at helping the learner become an effective, successful, and satisfied member of the workforce.


Master’s Degree: A learner with an appropriate Bachelor’s degree [1] or equivalent may earn this credential after two full-time semesters of approved study plus a large practicum. This is typically accomplished by taking the equivalent of 8 four-unit courses plus a 16-unit practicum. This is equivalent to 3 semesters of study in most universities. All master’s degrees at FJU are professional master’s degrees aimed at accelerating and advancing the learner’s professional career, competencies and capabilities.


Diploma: Individuals without a bachelor’s degree but with the necessary knowledge, skill, and discipline may earn a diploma credential after completing approved programs of study equivalent to a master’s degree.



[1] Not all undergraduate degree programs will properly prepare learners for FJU master’s programs and some learners may be required to take preparatory courses or provide a compelling portfolio before being eligible to join one of our master’s programs.
