Overview


Mobile App Development is one of the masters degree programs offerred by Franklin Jefferson University with the goal of creating experts in Android App Development. The curriculum of the program consists of a series of learning by doing projects designed to help students attain mastery in Android App Development. The list of seven projects to be completed is given below:











Course Projects: Creating a Mobile App for Store Front in 6 easy steps


BASIC LEVEL
PROJECT 1

Mockup Screens for Store Front

Develop a simple app that displays information about a small store.

Topics Covered:
Building Layouts, Applying Styles, Writng Views and ViewGroups, Learning XML

PROJECT 2

Load Products from Internet

Implement a feature in the app to load a list of products available in that store.


Topics Covered:
What is an API, Connecting and Parsing responses from an API, Updating content dynamically.

PROJECT 3

Search Products

Add a functionality to search through the list of products available in Store App.


Topics Covered:
Intents and Activities, Data Structures, Loops, and Custom Classes, Visual Polishing.


INTERMEDIATE LEVEL
PROJECT 4

Shopping Cart

Implement a shopping cart functionality to add products into the cart and also to remove them.


Topics Covered:
Intro to Databases, Intents and Activities, Custom Classes, User Sessions Handling.

PROJECT 5

Payment & Shipment Tracking

Integrate a payment screen into the Store App and also add a facility to track shipment.

Topics Covered:
Integrating Payment Gateway, Store the shipping details, Tracking Shipment.

PROJECT 6

Product Review and Ratings

Implement a Review and Ratings section which allows users to read and write reviews and give ratings.


Topics Covered:
Intro to Databases, Using SQLite Database to store and retrieve Data, Visual Polishing.

General Competency Test

What is General Competency Test?

General Competency Test is an Online proctored examination to evaluate your PUC level skills. This test consists of two components:

  • Aptitude Test
  • English Language Comprehension Test

Aptitude test is for 30 minutes and evaluates your quantitative, analytical, reasoning and verbal skills.
English Language comprehension Test is for 25 minutes and evaluates your capability of reading, listening, speaking and writing in English.


How do I take the Test?

This test is given through an online test portal. To take the exam you must register for the masters degree program. Link to the test portal and instructions to get started will be communicated through an email at registered email address.
Requirements:
